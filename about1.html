About Us

Sifu Ben Hill Bey began his marital arts training at the age of five with Shotokan Karate. At the age of twelve Ben purchased Robert Smith's book on Ba Gua Zhang and he tried teaching himself Ba Gua Zhang. One day in the park of the complex where he use to live in Middletown, New York, Sifu Ben was outside trying to walk the circle. An elderly Chinese gentleman walked up to him instructing on how to walk the circle properly with a simplistic change of direction. This began Sifu Bey's long road to learning Ba Gua Zhang. The single changing palm was ll he knew until meeting Sifu Kenny Gong at eighteen years of age.

Between the ages of twelve and eighteen he learned Northern Praying Mantis with Tony Chu and learned Eagle Claw with Lueng Shum. From Leung Shum he became skilled at Chin Na (Locking Skills). At twenty years old Sifu Bey decided to dedicate his life to the study of Ba Gua Zhang. Sifu Bey spent seventeen years with Sifu Kenny Gong. He met Chen Xian Ping in 1993 learning the Yin style of Cao Zhong Sheng. He also studied with Lou de Xue (Eric Lou) of the Gou style Ba Gua Zhang and Lin Chao Zhen of the Fu Style Ba Gua Zhang. In 1995 Sifu Ben also studied under Shi Pei Qi and Ha Jin Bao of the Yin Fu style of Ba Gua Zhang as thaught by Men Bao Zhen.

Sifu Bey has competed in fighting tournaments but was disqualified for utilizing realistic techniques as to oppossed point scoring techniques. He has also competed in various forms competitions. Sifu Bey is also a San Shou referee and judge certified by Xia Ba Hua of Beijing. He is the director of the Ba Gua Zhang Research and Boxing Association. He has endlessly worked to promote Ba Gua as a true martial art and not a dance. He was also the host of the Annual All Ba Gua Zhang Championship Tournament. His most recent work was authoring the book titled Ba Gua A Historical Analysis.

Sifu Lloyd Day began training in martial arts in 1975 under Charlie Hatchet in the Pai Lum system of Kung Fu until 1978. From 1979 through 1981 he trained in Tae Kwon Do under Master Hwang. In 1981 he trained in Shotokan Karate with Master Bo until the late 80's when he began learining under the late great Master Kenny Gong (Gong Ack Fei) of NYC who taught the internal arts of Shing Yi and Ba Gua Zhang. When Sifu Day was exposed to this system everything he has studied came together. Sifu Day studied under Sifu Gong until his passing in 1996. He continues to study and research the system daily as if it was his first day never lacking in zeal. Such is his dedication to the arts. His acute attention to details in the transimission of the arts, nature and spirit of the matter will bring great progress in students in any way of life. This is the accumalation of 39 years of experience.
